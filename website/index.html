Exhibit A

What a prompt becomes once it leaves.

Not an accusation — an inventory. This is the documented life cycle of text handed to a cloud service.

Stored

It persists after you close the tab

Providers retain prompts for varying windows — for abuse monitoring, product improvement, sometimes model training. Retention policies change; copies already made don't.

Compelled

It can be legally demanded

Anything a company holds can be subject to subpoenas, court orders, and preservation requests. Your chat history is discoverable the way email is — because legally, it's just business records.

Breached

It can leak like anything else

Every stored dataset is a future breach candidate. The safest data is data that was never collected — a placeholder can't be leaked back to you.

Correlated

It joins everything else known about you

A name here, an address there, a health question last month. Individually small; together, a profile. Redaction breaks the joins.

Exhibit B

The redaction procedure.

A small language model runs entirely on your device — multilingual entity detection, not English-only pattern matching. Three stages. Two of them never touch a network.

01 / Strike

The local model reads your draft and flags every identifying entity before anything is transmitted.

Hi, this is Anna Weber from Frankfurt, my email is a.weber@mail.de…
→

02 / Transmit

Only the redacted copy travels. The provider answers a prompt about placeholders — it never holds the originals.

Hi, this is [PERSON_1] from [PLACE_1], my email is [EMAIL_1]…
→

03 / Restore

The response returns, and the real values are swapped back in locally. Full answer for you; blanks for them.

Dear Anna Weber, regarding your request from Frankfurt…
